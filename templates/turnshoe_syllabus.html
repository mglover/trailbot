Turnshoe class syllabus

Tuition includes: all materials, basic toolkit (awl haft with a set of blades, skife), and in-class use of specialty tools. Students are encouraged to bring their own scissors, utility or xacto knife, ruler, pens, and sharpies if they have them. I will also have some on hand.

Week 1

  • Create model of your feet (cardboard, duct tape, nylon stockings, sharpies)
  • Convert the model to a paper pattern (kraft paper, scissors, xacto knife, ruler, hole punch, pens)

Week 2

  • Transfer pattern pieces to leather (paper pattern, pens)
  • Cut leather for uppers (6 oz. chrometan leather, leather shears
  • Cut leather for soles (9 oz. latigo leather, utility knife)
  • Sharpen your curved awl blade (file, curved awl)

Week 3

  • Prepare soles for sewing (file, stitch groover, plough, ruler, sharpie)
  • Prepare uppers for sewing (skife, ruler, pen, strap punch)
  • Stitch uppers -- continue as homework (straight awl, needles, bonded polyester thread)

Homework: finish stitching uppers

Week 4

  • Stitch uppers to soles (curved awl, machine stitching needle, nyltex thread)
  • Turn shoes rightside out

Week 5

  • Hammer shoes into finished shape (hammer, anvil)
  • Cut and install ankle strap (ruler, utility knife, 6 oz. chrometan leather)
